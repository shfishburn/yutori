This menu represents a personal culinary journey, combining elements from Italian, French, and Vietnamese cuisines. Each dish reflects techniques and ingredients gathered throughout my career in fine dining kitchens in Seattle.

From the precision of French technique to the soul of Italian tradition and the vibrant flavors of Vietnam, this prix fixe experience brings together the best of these distinct culinary worlds.

Prix Fixe Menu

Course Dish Description
Stuzzichini Fig and Speck Roasted figs wrapped in thin slices of Levoni speck, accompanied by creamy La Tur cheese and finished with a fermented pepper honey glaze. A perfect balance of sweet, salty, and creamy.
Insalata Roasted Cold Beet Salad Tender roasted beets dressed with a vibrant lime leaf, condensed milk, and Thai chili dressing. Garnished with fresh kumquat and herbaceous mint for brightness and acidity.
Antipasto Pickle and Cured Meat Sampler A selection of house-pickled vegetables including carrots, daikon, beets, and jalapeños, served alongside premium cured meats: Levoni Speck, Bresaola, and Fermin Jamon Serrano. Presented with fresh pea vines.
Palate Cleanser Whiskey Bone Broth Shot House-made rich beef bone broth fortified with quality whiskey (Buffalo Trace, Woodford Reserve, or Four Roses). Served warm in a ceramic cup, this savory interlude prepares the palate for the main course.
Entrée Pistachio Crusted Black Cod with Beurre Blanc Delicate black cod with a crisp herb-pistachio crust, served with a classic French beurre blanc sauce enhanced with charred lemon and sticky chicken stock. Accompanied by broccolini finished with yuzu kosho for an umami accent.
Mignardises Fermented Pepper Honey Gnocco Fritto Traditional Italian fried dough pillows, light and airy, drizzled with a complex fermented garlic-pepper honey. An addictive sweet-savory bite that bridges the gap between savory and sweet courses.
Dessert Blackberry Sage Sorbetto Intensely flavored blackberry sorbetto infused with fresh sage. The smooth, dairy-free frozen dessert is garnished with crispy fried sage leaves, creating a sophisticated but refreshing finale to the meal.

Timeline for Prep

  • 2+ Days Before: Start fermented garlic honey
  • Day Before: Prepare gnocco fritto dough, blackberry sorbetto
  • Morning Of: Make stocks, prep vegetables, portion cheese
  • Service: Finish cooking proteins, make beurre blanc to order

Fermentation
1+ Week Before

  • Fermented Garlic Honey: Cover clean garlic cloves in raw honey, let sit in flip top jar, cover loosely.

The Day Before
1+ Day Before

  • Beef for Broth: Hard sear short rib and top round chunks, simmer with mirepoix, star anise, cinnamon, and cardamom for at least 2 hours.
  • Gnocco Fritto: Prep dough day before using Italian 00 flour, sugar, instant yeast, salt, whole milk, and lard. Store in freezer, fry to order.
  • Blackberry Sorbetto:
    1. In a blender, purée blackberries until smooth
    2. Strain blackberry mixture through a fine mesh strainer and push it through with a rubber spatula
    3. Discard the remaining seeds and solids
    4. Combine water, sugar, and sage
    5. Heat mixture until it just begins to simmer, stirring until sugar is dissolved
    6. Remove from heat and let steep, uncovered, for 30 minutes
    7. Strain sugar syrup into a large bowl
    8. Stir blackberry puree and lemon juice until evenly mixed
    9. Freeze overnight

Stocks & Broths
Morning of Service

  • Sticky Chicken Stock: Roast chicken carcass and feet until deeply browned, simmer in water and skim the scum off the top until completely clear. Add mirepoix and simmer for 45 min.

Proteins & Garnish
Morning of Service

  • Cod: Salt in advance to pull as much moisture as possible and pat dry. Par-cook on parchment paper. Pull and flip fish, add pistachio crumb and finish until the crumb is hot and fish is cooked. Meanwhile prepare Beurre Blanc.
  • Cured Meats: Slice thinly on deli slicer:
    • Levoni Speck (slice extra for figs and gnocco fritto)
    • Bresaola
    • Fermin Jamon Serrano
  • Fried Sage: Pick leaves and fry prior to service, set aside on paper towel.
  • Prepare Pea Vines: Clean and trim for garnishing the cured meat platter.

Sauces & Dressings
Morning of Service

  • Beurre Blanc sauce: Make to order with:
    • Charred lemon (grilled half lemon juiced)
    • Quality butter (cut into pads)
    • White wine (squeeze bottle)
    • Shallot (brunoise)
  • Fermented Pepper Honey: Use as glue to wrap figs.
  • Dressing: Combine lime leaf, lime juice, condensed milk, extra virgin olive oil, salt, sugar, MSG and blend.
  • Pickle Brine: 1:1:0.75 Vinegar Water Sugar

Vegetables & Garnishes
Morning of Service

  • Figs: Cut in half, toss with salt, pepper and EVOO, roast in oven 400 degrees for 5 min, let sit at room temperature.
  • Beets:
    1. Boil beets until al dente, pull from water, pat dry and toss with salt, pepper and EVOO
    2. Finish cooking in 500 degree oven, cool down
    3. Cut into wedges
  • Broccolini: Toss in salt & pepper and EVOO. Cook in 500 degree oven until al dente. Torch or grill for char and to fully cook. Toss in butter and yuzu kosho.
  • Kumquat: Slice thinly lengthwise.
  • Red Thai Chili: Slice thinly on a bias.
  • Chrysanthemum/Celery Leaf: Pick nice leaves for service.
  • Carrots: Cut into spears.
  • Daikon: Cut into batons.
  • Jalapeño: Thinly slice.
  • Fried Sage: Pick leaves and fry prior to service; set aside on paper towel.

Components & Mixtures
Before Service

  • La Tur: Portion into 1.5 oz.
  • Levoni Speck: Portion to wrap figs.
  • Pistachio Crumb: Crush whole pistachios in a Robot Coupe, combine with toasted bread crumb, fresh basil (chiffonade), fresh thyme (chiffonade), fresh parsley (chiffonade), grated Parmigiano Reggiano, garlic.

Bar Prep
Before Service
