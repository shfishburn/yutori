Body Composition Details

This section shows your current body composition—including your weight, fat mass, lean mass, and BF%—which is used to set realistic goals.

Forecast Charts Explanation

The charts display your predicted weight loss trajectory, daily calorie target, and macronutrient needs over time. They update dynamically as your metabolic adaptation is simulated.

How Are These Figures Calculated?

Settings: Your current lean mass, age, and gender are used to compute your BMR (370 + 21.6 × [Lean Mass in kg]). TDEE is derived by multiplying BMR by your activity level, and your goal weight range is calculated using your target BF percentages.

Goal Range Explanation

Your goal weight range is determined by your lean mass and your target BF percentages:
Goal Weight Low = Lean Mass ÷ (1 – pLow) and Goal Weight High = Lean Mass ÷ (1 – pHigh). For example, a reference user might have:
Weight: 218.35 lbs–232.00 lbs, Fat Mass: 32.75 lbs–46.40 lbs, Lean Mass: 185.60 lbs, BF%: 15.0%–20.0% (GOOD).

Calories & Macronutrients Explanation

Your BMR is computed as 370 + 21.6 × (Lean Mass in kg) and multiplied by your activity factor to derive TDEE. A relative deficit is applied to set your daily calorie target. We use a dynamic effective energy density: E_eff = L × 760 + (1 – L) × 3500, where L is the fraction of weight loss that is lean tissue. This model dynamically updates your macronutrient targets.

Weight Goal

Lose, Maintain, or Gain Lean Mass. This adjusts your final daily calories.

Dietary Approach

Low-carb, High-protein, or Balanced macros determine your nutrient split.

Activity Level

Your activity multiplier adjusts your BMR. Examples: Sedentary ~1.2, Very Active ~1.725.

Calorie Adjustment Info

Depending on your goal, choose from these options:

Maintenance: 0 cal/day (no change)

Weight Loss: Options for a 250, 500, 750, or 1000 cal/day deficit.

Weight Gain: Options for a 125, 250–500, 500–750, or 750+ cal/day surplus.

Lean Goal (%)

This indicates the percentage of weight change that is lean tissue loss versus fat loss.

Fat% Range

Select a target body-fat range (for example, "excellent" ~10–15% BF).
